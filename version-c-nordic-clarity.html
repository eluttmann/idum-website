The Hidden Crisis

The Silent Threat Beneath Our Oceans

As early as 1910, the dumping of chemical and conventional munitions was recorded in the English Channel. This process continued throughout all seas as a cheap means of disposal for militaries until the mid-1970s when countries began to understand the environmental catastrophe they were creating.

1.6 million tons of underwater munitions—chemical, biological, and conventional—are rusting in 54 seas and oceans. Earth's five major ocean gyres circulate water and contaminants across all hemispheres. These slow-moving conveyor belts do not respect borders.

Chemical Contamination
Munitions' chemical breakdown is spreading deadly arsenic, mustard gas compounds, and lewisite degradation products across the sea floor.
Unstable Ordnance
Unexploded ordnance is becoming more unstable with time, posing acute risks to fishing fleets and offshore infrastructure.
Marine Ecosystem Collapse
Bioaccumulation in fish, coral bleaching, and dead zones near dump sites threaten food security for billions.
Ocean Gyre Dispersion
Contaminants from one region propagate through global ocean circulation, affecting ecosystems thousands of miles away.
Radiological Hazards
Radiological weapons dumped at sea compound the crisis, creating compound stressors on ecosystems.

Terrance P. Long CD.

Global Authority on Underwater & Submerged Munitions

Terrance (Terry) P. Long is the Chair and founder of the International Dialogues on Underwater Munitions (IDUM) and a world-leading expert on sea-dumped chemical and conventional weapons. A retired Canadian Military Engineer with over 30 years of experience specializing in Explosive Ordnance Disposal (EOD), Terrance has led international efforts to address the environmental and human health impacts of underwater and submerged munitions globally.

As the driving force behind UN Ocean Action #21356, Terrance has established the international framework for underwater munitions responses. His advocacy led to the landmark Baltic Sea blasting moratorium, creating a global model for environmental protection.

UN Ocean Action #21356

A Formal International Commitment

UN Ocean Action #21356 represents the formal international commitment to address underwater munitions on a global scale. Led by Terrance P. Long CD., this initiative provides the framework for cooperation across national boundaries.

1
International Dialogues with Global Stakeholders
Bringing together governments, military, scientists, and NGOs across national borders.
2
Comprehensive Global Munitions Map
Documenting locations, types, and quantities across all 54 affected seas.
3
Risk-Ranked Cleanup Priorities
Focusing resources ranked by environmental risk and human health impact.
4
Standardized Protocols & Technologies
Establishing best practices for safe, effective remediation globally.
5
Training & Certification Programs
Preparing the next generation of specialists to restore resilient oceans.
