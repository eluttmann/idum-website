The Hidden Crisis

The Silent Threat Beneath Our Oceans

As early as 1910, the dumping of chemical and conventional munitions was recorded in the English Channel. This process continued throughout all seas as a cheap means of disposal for militaries until the mid-1970s when countries began to understand the environmental catastrophe they were creating.

1.6 million tons of underwater munitions—chemical, biological, and conventional—are rusting in 54 seas and oceans. Earth's five major ocean gyres circulate water and contaminants across all hemispheres. These slow-moving conveyor belts do not respect borders.

Evidence shows cancer rates up to 5x higher in coastal populations near known munitions sites. Shellfish toxicity linked to mustard gas and lewisite degradation has been documented. TNT with a 5,000-year half-life bioaccumulates in fish and coral.

Chemical Contamination
Munitions' chemical breakdown is spreading deadly arsenic, mustard gas compounds, and lewisite degradation products across the sea floor and into the food chain.
Unstable Ordnance
Unexploded ordnance is becoming more unstable with time, posing acute risks to fishing fleets, offshore energy infrastructure, and submarine cables.
Marine Ecosystem Collapse
Bioaccumulation in fish, coral bleaching, and dead zones near dump sites threaten food security for billions of people who depend on healthy oceans.
Ocean Gyre Dispersion
There is no "away" on Spaceship Earth. Contaminants from one region propagate through global ocean circulation, affecting ecosystems thousands of miles away.
Radiological Hazards
Radiological weapons dumped at sea compound the crisis, intersecting with radioactive water releases and creating compound stressors on already-stressed ecosystems.
